For many of you, a degree in Computer Science will be the stepping stone to a career in software engineering. At the same time, most of your experience building software has been to write some Java code on your own, compile it directly with the Java compiler, and run it on your own machine. Real software engineering is very different.

In this module, we will explore the gap between the programming you will have done in your first year and the real software engineering you are likely to do in your careers. You will be introduced to the different contexts that software engineering takes place in and the different processes that organisations may employ surrounding it. Furthermore, you will gain foundational knowledge of relevant tools and technical skills.

Optional Slack team & How to get in touch

There is an optional Slack team for this module. Slack is a tool for real-time collaboration, similar to Microsoft Teams, that is commonly used in industry. Due to its use in industry, I have set up a Slack team for this module that we can use to quickly discuss your questions more effectively than via email. You can sign up for an account on it (note that you cannot and should not try to log in with your Birmingham password). You can use this to ask questions about lectures, about the coursework, or software engineering more generally in one of the channels. You can also send me direct messages with your questions. For all this, you can choose to remain anonymous as well as you are not required to sign up with your real name. Participation is entirely optional and all key information related to the module will be communicated via other channels, such as this website or email.

If you prefer not to use Slack, you can reach me with questions at any time at m.gale@bham.ac.uk.
